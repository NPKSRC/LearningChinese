# Chinese Lesson Deck — User Guide

A pocket study app for the Batch 01 华语 vocabulary (Chinese ⇄ ไทย). It runs in any
web browser, works offline once loaded, and saves your progress on your own device.

---

## 1. Getting started

### On a phone (recommended)
1. Open `ChineseLessonDeck.html` in your mobile browser (Safari on iPhone, Chrome on Android).
2. Add it to your home screen so it opens full-screen like a real app:
   - **iPhone / Safari:** tap the **Share** button → **Add to Home Screen** → **Add**.
   - **Android / Chrome:** tap the **⋮** menu → **Add to Home screen** (or **Install app**).
3. Launch it from the new icon. The browser bars disappear and it behaves like a native app.

### On a computer
Just double-click the file, or drag it into a browser tab. Everything works the same.

> **Note on saved progress:** your study history is tied to the browser/device you use.
> If you open the app on a different phone or clear your browser data, progress starts fresh.

---

## 2. The four tabs

The bottom bar switches between the four areas of the app.

### 📕 Study — flashcards with spaced repetition
This is the core of the app and the most effective way to memorize.

- A card shows one word. **Tap the card to flip it** and reveal the answer (Pinyin, Thai
  meaning, English gloss, and an example sentence where one exists).
- After flipping, rate how well you knew it:

  | Button | Use it when… | Effect |
  |--------|--------------|--------|
  | **Again** | You didn't know it | Comes back within the same session |
  | **Hard** | You barely got it | Returns soon (short interval) |
  | **Good** | You knew it | Returns after a normal interval |
  | **Easy** | It was effortless | Pushed far into the future |

  Each rating reschedules the word automatically. Words you find hard appear more often;
  words you know well drift further apart, so your review time goes where it's needed.

- The small label under each button (e.g. `3d`) previews when you'd next see the card.
- A **session** is a batch of due cards plus up to 12 new ones. When you finish, you'll see
  a completion screen — tap **Study again** for another round.

### 📝 Quiz — test yourself
A 10-question mix drawn from your lesson set:

- **Multiple choice** — see a Chinese word, pick the correct Thai meaning (or the reverse,
  depending on your direction toggle).
- **Fill in the blank** — the original warm-up questions from your worksheets, with their
  A / B / C choices and the Thai translation shown as a hint.

Correct answers turn green, wrong answers red, and the right choice is always highlighted so
you learn from mistakes. Your score and a percentage appear at the end.

### 📋 Browse — look up any word
- A searchable list of all 105 words.
- Search box matches **汉字, Pinyin, Thai, or English** — type any of them.
- **Tap a row to hear the word spoken** in Chinese.
- The small tag on the right shows which lesson the word came from.

### 📊 Stats — track progress
- **Words started** — how many you've reviewed at least once.
- **Mastered** — words with a review interval of 7+ days (well into long-term memory).
- **Due to review** — words ready for another look right now.
- **Quiz accuracy** — your lifetime correct-answer rate, with the number of questions taken.
- **Reset all progress** — wipes your history on this device (asks for confirmation first).

## 3. Controls you'll use often

### Direction toggle (top right)
Switch between **中→ไทย** (see Chinese, recall the meaning) and **ไทย→中** (see Thai,
recall the Chinese word). This changes both the flashcards and the quiz, so you can practice
both recognition and production.

### Lesson chips (top of the Study tab)
Tap **All** to study everything, or a single date (**8.10, 8.17, 8.24, 9.07, 9.21, 9.28**)
to drill just that lesson. The choice also scopes your quizzes.

### Audio 🔊
The speaker button (on cards and browse rows) reads the Chinese aloud using your device's
built-in text-to-speech. Most phones include a Chinese voice. If yours doesn't, the button
stays silent and everything else still works — you can add a Chinese voice in your phone's
language/accessibility settings.

---

## 4. A suggested routine

1. Pick one lesson and run a **Study** session, rating honestly.
2. Once the cards feel familiar, switch to **Quiz** for that lesson to test recall.
3. Flip the **direction toggle** and study again to practice producing the Chinese.
4. Check **Stats** every few days and clear whatever is **Due to review**.
5. Use **Browse** any time you want to look something up or hear pronunciation.

Short, daily sessions beat long, occasional ones — the spaced-repetition schedule is built
around coming back regularly.

---

## 5. Good to know

- **Offline:** after the first load, the app runs without internet. (The web fonts and the
  audio voice are the only things that benefit from a connection.)
- **Privacy:** nothing is uploaded. All progress stays in your browser's local storage.
- **Content source:** built from `Batch01_Chinese_Lesson_Master_v0_2.xlsx` — 105 vocabulary
  items, 39 with example sentences, plus 5 fill-in-the-blank questions across 6 lessons.
- **Example sentences in Thai** were not present in this batch, so example sentences currently
  show Chinese + Pinyin only. They'll appear automatically if later files include them.
